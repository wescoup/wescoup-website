Alternative Hypothesis for Cosmological Redshift

A different look at the source of cosmological redshift based on energy conservation.

Publication: viXra:2411.0175

The Hypothesis: IGM Energy Transfer

This hypothesis suggests an alternative to the "expanding universe" model, proposing that photons lose energy to the **Intergalactic Medium (IGM)** during their travel.

The core mechanism is based on the conservation of energy, where $\Delta E_{\text{IGM}} = -\Delta E_{\text{photon}}$, resulting in the observed redshift following an exponential decay model ($A(t) = A_0(1-r)^t$).

Distinction from "Tired Light"

While related to historical "Tired Light" concepts, this model differs by proposing a specific mechanism of energy transfer with the IGM and providing a quantitative, testable model for the energy loss rate ($r$).
